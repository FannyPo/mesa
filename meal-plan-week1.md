# 7-Day Meal Plan — Prediabetic Working Mom
**Family of 4 | $75 weekly budget | Blood-sugar safe | Zero food waste**

---

## How This Plan Works

Three things make this different:

1. **Every ingredient is used completely.** The most perishable items (spinach, broccoli, avocado) are used in the first three days. Cabbage, carrots, and frozen items cover the back half of the week. Nothing goes soft in the back of the fridge.

2. **Sunday prep does most of the work.** One hour on Sunday means every dinner this week takes 15 minutes of active cooking or less.

3. **The blood sugar swaps are consistent.** You already know ramen spikes blood sugar. The same thing happens with white rice, white bread, and instant oatmeal — same mechanism, different shape. This plan applies that rule across the whole week, not just for one food. Where your family eats rice, you eat a smaller portion of brown rice or a swap. They eat the same dinner. You get the better version of it.

---

## SUNDAY PREP — Do This Before the Week Starts (1 hour)

**1. Bake all 8 chicken thighs**
Preheat oven to 400°F. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Bake 38–40 minutes. Let cool.
- Keep 2 thighs whole → Monday dinner
- Shred meat off remaining 6 thighs with two forks → refrigerate in a covered container. This becomes lunch and Friday dinner.
- Yield: about 4 cups shredded chicken

**2. Cook the brown rice**
Rinse 1 lb dry rice. Cook per package instructions (~40 min). Divide into a container and refrigerate. This is your rice for the whole week.

That's it. Everything else this week takes 15 minutes or less the day of.

## THE WEEK

---

### MONDAY

**Breakfast — Oatmeal with cinnamon** *(5 min)*

½ cup rolled oats per person + 1 cup water each. Stovetop: boil, then low heat 5 minutes stirring. Microwave: 2 minutes. Stir in ½ tsp cinnamon per bowl. Top with a spoonful of frozen blueberries if you want something sweet — they thaw in 2 minutes.

> **Important:** Buy rolled oats (old-fashioned), not instant. Same aisle, same brand, different label. Instant oats are processed more and spike blood sugar almost as fast as white bread. Rolled oats take 3 extra minutes. That difference matters.

---

**Lunch — Chickpea and cabbage bowl** *(pack Sunday night — 4 min)*

Drain 1 can chickpeas. Toss with ½ cup salsa and a handful of shredded cabbage. Pack in a container with a lid.
Kids: same thing, add cheese on top.
Refrigerate overnight. Grab it in the morning. Eat cold or microwave 90 seconds.

---

**Dinner — Baked chicken + roasted broccoli + brown rice** *(20 min, mostly hands-off)*

- Chicken: the 2 whole thighs from Sunday. Reheat at 350°F for 12 min, or microwave covered 3–4 min.
- Broccoli: cut into florets, toss with olive oil and salt. Roast at 400°F, 20 min. Or microwave 4 min — works fine, just less crispy.
- Rice: scoop from Sunday's container. Microwave 90 seconds.
- **Your plate:** ½ cup brown rice. **Kids:** ¾ cup is fine.

*Tonight before bed:* Nothing to prep. Tomorrow's breakfast is 2 minutes.

---

### TUESDAY

**Breakfast — Greek yogurt + blueberries** *(2 min, no cooking)*

¾ cup plain Greek yogurt per adult. Handful of frozen blueberries on top — they thaw while you eat.
Kids: oatmeal (quick batch, 5 min) or whatever they normally eat in the morning.

> Plain Greek yogurt has twice the protein of regular yogurt and half the sugar. It digests slowly. On a morning where breakfast competes with everything else, this takes less time than driving through anywhere.

---

**Lunch — Chicken and avocado bowl** *(pack Monday night — 3 min)*

Shredded chicken (from Sunday) + ½ avocado sliced + shredded cabbage + squeeze of lime + 2 Tbsp salsa. Pack in a container.
Kids: add cheese or extra salsa.
Pack tonight. Refrigerate. Grab it tomorrow.

---

**Dinner — Turkey tacos** *(15 min)* ✓ The whole family eats this

- Cook ½ lb ground turkey in a skillet over medium heat. Break it up as it cooks. Season with 1 tsp cumin + 1 tsp chili powder (or 1 taco seasoning packet). About 10 minutes.
- Drain and warm 1 can black beans in the microwave, 2 min.
- Set up the taco bar: warm whole wheat tortillas, turkey, black beans, shredded cabbage, salsa, cheese.
- **Your plate:** 2 tacos. Load up on turkey + beans + cabbage. Cabbage adds crunch and uses up what you bought. Skip extra rice on the side.
- **Kids:** let them build their own. They will.

> Why this works for blood sugar: protein from turkey + fiber from beans + cabbage slows absorption. Whole wheat tortilla has less blood sugar impact than a side of white rice.

*Tonight before bed:* Set oats and water in a pot so it's ready to heat tomorrow morning. Or make overnight oats: ½ cup oats + ½ cup water in a jar in the fridge.

---

### WEDNESDAY

**Breakfast — Scrambled eggs + sautéed spinach** *(8 min)*

8 eggs scrambled for the family (2 per adult, 1–2 per kid). While eggs cook, wilt 2 cups spinach in the same pan with garlic powder. Serve together.

Most filling breakfast in the plan. Protein + iron, nothing that spikes blood sugar.

---

**Lunch — Leftover taco bowl** *(pack Tuesday night — 3 min)*

Yesterday's turkey + remaining black beans + ½ cup brown rice from Sunday. Heat and pack. Add cold cabbage shreds on top.

---

**Dinner — Zucchini and black bean quesadillas** *(15 min)* ✓ Kids will eat this

- Slice 2 zucchini into thin rounds. Sauté with olive oil and garlic, 5 min until soft.
- Mash ½ can black beans (the second can) with a fork.
- Spread beans on one half of a whole wheat tortilla. Add zucchini, cheese. Fold.
- Press in a dry skillet on medium heat, 3 min per side until golden.
- Makes 6 quesadillas. Cut into wedges. Serve with salsa.

---

### THURSDAY

**Breakfast — Oatmeal with cinnamon** *(5 min)*

Same as Monday. ½ cup rolled oats per person, cinnamon, blueberries on top if any remain.

---

**Lunch — Chicken and spinach bowl** *(pack Wednesday night — 4 min)*

Remaining shredded chicken + remaining spinach. If spinach is starting to wilt: sauté it Wednesday night with garlic, let cool, pack cold Thursday. Still good in a bowl.
Add salsa. Pack in a container.

> Use the spinach today. It's Thursday — this is the last day it's good.

---

**Dinner — Ground turkey and vegetable stir-fry** *(15 min)*

- Brown remaining ½ lb ground turkey in a skillet.
- Add the full bag of frozen mixed vegetables straight from frozen. Add 2 Tbsp soy sauce + garlic. Stir and cook 8 min.
- Microwave the bag of frozen cauliflower rice per package (usually 4 min).
- **Your plate:** cauliflower rice base, stir-fry on top.
- **Kids' plates:** remaining brown rice from Sunday, same stir-fry on top.

> Cauliflower rice has about 5g carbs per cup. Regular white rice has 45g. Same texture under the stir-fry. You don't have to announce the swap.

*Tonight before bed:* Hard-boil 4 eggs. Cover with cold water, bring to boil, turn off heat, lid on for 10 minutes. Refrigerate. Tomorrow's lunch is already done.

---

### FRIDAY

**Breakfast — Greek yogurt + carrot sticks** *(1 min)*

¾ cup Greek yogurt per adult. Raw carrot sticks from the bag, rinsed. Nothing to cook. Nothing to pack.

---

**Lunch — Hard-boiled eggs + carrot sticks** *(already done)*

2 eggs per person + carrot sticks. Pack in a container and go.

This lunch is simple on purpose. Friday is the hardest day to stay on plan. Making it require zero prep Thursday night means there's nothing to forget.

---

**Dinner — Sweet potato and chicken bowl** *(10 min active)* ✓ Whole family eats this

- Pierce 2–3 sweet potatoes with a fork. Microwave 8–10 min, turning once. (Or oven 400°F, 45 min.)
- Warm remaining shredded chicken in a pan or microwave.
- Warm remaining ½ can black beans.
- Cut sweet potatoes in half. One half per person. Top with chicken, black beans, salsa, and a spoonful of Greek yogurt instead of sour cream.

> Sweet potato has a glycemic index of ~50. White potato is ~80. White rice is ~73. It tastes slightly sweet, the family will eat it, and the protein + beans slow blood sugar absorption further.

---

### SATURDAY

**Breakfast — Scrambled eggs** *(7 min)*

6–8 eggs for the family. Serve with carrot sticks or any remaining blueberries.

---

**Lunch — Refrigerator clearout** *(no cooking)*

Open the fridge. Eat what's left. Any chicken scraps, beans, rice, stir-fry vegetables — reheat together or eat cold. This is not a recipe. It's the meal that keeps you from throwing away food you already paid for.

---

**Dinner — Egg and vegetable fried cauliflower rice** *(15 min)*

This meal uses everything left. Nothing goes in the trash.

- Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high.
- Add remaining shredded cabbage + remaining carrots (shredded or sliced thin). Cook 5 min.
- Push vegetables to the side. Crack 4 eggs into the pan. Scramble them into the vegetables.
- Add remaining frozen cauliflower rice or brown rice. Add 2 Tbsp soy sauce + garlic. Stir everything together. Cook 8 min on medium-high.

---

### SUNDAY

**Breakfast — Veggie omelette** *(10 min)*

2–3 eggs per adult. Add any remaining frozen vegetables and cheese. Cook, fold, done.

Then go shopping and start again.

|---|---|---|
| Spinach | Zucchini | Cabbage |
| Broccoli | Any remaining spinach | Carrots |
| Avocado | | Sweet potatoes |
| | | Frozen items |
| | | Eggs + Greek yogurt |

If you skip a meal or swap days, just check this column. Whatever spoils fastest goes first.

---

## WHEN THE PLAN BREAKS DOWN

It will break down at least once. That is not failure — it is Tuesday.

When it does:
- Hard-boiled eggs are in the fridge. Two eggs take 30 seconds to eat.
- Chickpeas + salsa + cabbage in a container: 90 seconds.
- Greek yogurt straight from the container: 0 seconds.
- If you're ordering out: grilled chicken + salad at any fast-casual place. No white rice bowls. If the rice comes with it, eat half.

The goal is not a perfect week. It's a better week than the one before it.
